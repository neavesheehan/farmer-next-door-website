Frequently asked questions

 

What is Farmer Next Door?

An app connecting buyers and sellers of homegrown and homemade food — eggs, veg, honey, dairy, baked goods and more — in the Chew Valley and South Bristol area. Browse a map of nearby sellers, message them, and arrange a pickup.

Is it free to use?

Yes, the app itself is free for both buyers and sellers. Any payment for food is arranged directly between buyer and seller — the app doesn't process payments.

Does the app handle delivery?

No — Farmer Next Door is for arranging pickups only. You agree a time and place with the seller in chat, then collect in person.

How do I know food is safe?

Sellers are responsible for complying with UK food hygiene and labelling law. We don't inspect or verify sellers — if you're ever unsure about a seller's food, ask them directly before agreeing to buy. See our Terms & Conditions for more detail.

Where does the app work?

The app is focused on the Chew Valley and South Bristol area, where the map defaults to. You can still browse and message sellers further afield if they're listed.

How do I become a seller?

Sign up in the app and choose "Seller" — you'll set up your shop details, categories, availability and a price list. If you're regularly selling food to the public, you should also register as a food business with your local authority (it's free — see food.gov.uk).

How do I get in touch?

You can reach the founder using the contact details provided at sign-up events, or via the app's own contact information.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026
This policy was researched and drafted by the founder to reflect UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018 as we understand them, but has not been reviewed by a qualified solicitor. If Farmer Next Door grows beyond a small local community, professional legal review is still strongly recommended.

1. Who we are

Farmer Next Door is a small local project, and its founder is the "data controller" responsible for your personal data under UK data protection law. You can contact us using the details provided at sign-up events, or via the app's contact information, including to exercise any of the rights below.

2. What we collect, and why

  • Account details — name, username, email, phone number, and (for sellers) category tags and availability. Used to create your account and let buyers and sellers identify and contact each other. Legal basis: necessary to provide the service you've signed up for.
  • Location — a general home area for buyers (to centre the map near you) and a specific pickup location for sellers (so buyers can find them). Your device will ask for your permission before we can access precise location — you can decline and type an address instead. Legal basis: your consent, given through that permission prompt, and — for sellers — necessary to provide the service (a pickup location has to be shown somewhere for buyers to find).
  • Messages and order proposals — stored so both sides of a conversation can see their chat history, and so pickups can be tracked. Legal basis: necessary to provide the messaging and pickup features.
  • Photos you upload — profile/shop photos and price list item photos — to display on your profile or listings. Legal basis: your consent (you choose whether to upload one).
  • Ratings and comments — to help other users choose who to buy from or sell to. Legal basis: our legitimate interest in maintaining trust on the platform.
  • Online status — whether you currently have the app open, shown to people you're chatting with. Legal basis: necessary to provide that feature; this only reflects live app usage, not location.
  • Favourites — sellers you've saved, visible only to you. Legal basis: your consent (you choose to save someone).
  • Push notification token — a device identifier used to deliver notifications for new messages and pickup confirmations, generated after you grant notification permission. Legal basis: your consent, given through that permission prompt.

3. How we use it

We use this information only to run the app: showing sellers on a map, enabling chat between buyers and sellers, tracking confirmed pickups, sending pickup reminder notifications, and showing ratings. We don't use your data for advertising, and we don't sell it to third parties.

4. Who can see what

Your display name, photo, category tags, availability, pickup location (sellers), and ratings are visible to other signed-in app users. Your phone number and email are not shown publicly — messaging happens inside the app. Chat messages, favourites, and your exact home area (buyers) are only visible to you and, where relevant, the other person in that conversation.

5. Where it's stored, and who else sees it

Data is stored using Supabase, a hosted database and file storage provider, who process it on our behalf under their own data processing terms. Push notifications are delivered via Expo's push notification service (operated by Expo/650 Industries) and, for iOS devices, Apple's own push notification service — both receive your device's push token and the notification text (for example, "New message from Jamie") in order to deliver it, but not your full message content, location, or other account details. We don't use any other third-party services with access to your personal data.

6. How long we keep it

We keep your data for as long as your account is active. If you ask us to delete your account (see below), we'll delete your personal data within a reasonable time, except where we need to keep limited records to meet a legal obligation or resolve a dispute.

7. Your rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Ask for a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Ask us to correct inaccurate data.
  • Ask us to delete your account and associated data.
  • Ask us to restrict or object to certain processing.
  • Ask for your data in a portable format.
  • Complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ico.org.uk) if you think we've mishandled your data.

To exercise any of these, contact the app's founder via the details provided at sign-up events. We'll action requests as soon as reasonably possible.

8. Children

Farmer Next Door is intended for users aged 18 and over, in line with our Terms & Conditions, and isn't knowingly used to collect data from children.

9. Security

We take reasonable steps to protect your data, including relying on Supabase's access controls so that, for example, your private messages are only readable by you and the other person in that conversation. No system is completely secure, so we can't guarantee absolute security.

10. Changes

As this is an early version of the app, this policy may be updated as the service develops. We'll update the "last updated" date above when we do.

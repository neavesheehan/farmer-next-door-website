2. What Farmer Next Door is — and isn't

Farmer Next Door is a platform that helps buyers and sellers find and message each other nearby, and keep track of pickups they've agreed on. We are not a party to any sale — under the Consumer Rights Act 2015, the seller (not us) is the trader responsible for the goods they sell. We do not buy, sell, handle, inspect, store, or deliver any food ourselves, and we do not act as agent for either side. Any contract for the sale of goods is formed directly between the buyer and seller, and is governed by whatever they agree between themselves.

If you sell food through the app, you are legally responsible for complying with UK food law as it applies to you, including:

  • Registering as a food business with your local authority at least 28 days before you start selling, where required (this is free — see the Food Standards Agency's guidance at food.gov.uk).
  • Any extra rules for the specific food you sell — for example, eggs, meat, and dairy have their own registration thresholds and labelling rules (the Food Standards Agency and Animal and Plant Health Agency publish current guidance on this).
  • Following good food hygiene practice, and being honest and accurate about what you're selling, including any allergens.
  • Considering appropriate product and public liability insurance — this isn't required to use the app, but is sensible if you're regularly selling food to the public.

We don't check or verify sellers' compliance with any of the above, and listing on the app is not a claim by us that a seller meets these requirements.
